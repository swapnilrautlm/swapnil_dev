The companion to the Flight Plan. The how is one repeatable template; the calendar below just tells her which skill to aim it at each day, all the way to September 12.

The 2-hour template — use it every day

Summer weekday · per subject

2 hours, two blocks

  1. 5 min — re-work yesterday's logged misses.
  2. 35 min — Question Bank set: today's skill, ~10–12 questions at the target difficulty.
  3. 15 min — check answers, read every explanation, log misses to the Google Sheet.
  4. 10 min — break.
  5. 35 min — second set: next difficulty up, or the paired skill, ~10–12 questions.
  6. 15 min — review + log + jot the pattern behind today's misses.
≈ 20–24 questions / subject
School weekday (from Sep 2) · per subject

1 hour, one block

  1. 5 min — re-work yesterday's misses.
  2. 35 min — one set of ~10 questions on the day's skill.
  3. 15 min — check, read explanations, log.
≈ 10 questions / subject
Weekend · light, 2 hrs max

Recover & consolidate

  1. Re-work the week's logged misses.
  2. 15–20 min English vocabulary from context errors.
  3. One short mixed set — no new heavy content.
Rest is part of the plan
Question volume. The bank holds 1,756 Math and 1,688 Reading & Writing questions. At ~20 per subject on summer weekdays that's roughly 900 of each across the summer — deliberately leaving the rest as a fresh reservoir for the school-year weeks and re-drills. The six Bluebook tests supply the timed, adaptive reps on top.
